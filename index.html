Seawind Catamarans
 Australia's most popular cruising sail and power catamarans

With 27 years experience building mullihulls. Seawind’s Richard Ward wanted to build a cruising catamaran specifically designed for serious blue water cruising. The result was the Seawind 1250, 41ft of the best ideas, engineering and craftsmanship accumulated by his team of expert builders. However, before releasing the new model to production, Richard wanted to put the boat through Its paces and personally sailed the first Seawind 1250 across the notorious Bass Strait to test her seaworthiness. Impressed by the boats performance, but still not satisfied.Seawind’s delivery skipper was ordered to sail the 1250 across the Tasman Sea to New Zealand and back, over some of the worst sea conditions in the world and do his best to -Break It” , After successfully completing a 6,000 NM ocean sea trial, Richard is finally satisfied .

Now you can cruise In the confidence that Seawind has pushed the limits to produce a boat that will take you to the limits.

Weta Trimarans

Lightweight and simple, the WETA can be rigged and beach launched by a single person in 20 minutes.
The WETA trimaran is a great performing 4.4 m or 14'5" triamaran. Only a WETA can be sailed with one, two or three sails blazing by one, two or three people!

No wonder it was Boat of the Year in 2010!

WindRider Trimarans

The WindRider Trimarans let anyone enjoy the thrill of performance sailing. Simple to assemble and rig, it's also exceptionally easy to sail. The Windrider 17 is an incredibly easy boat to sail that is all but impossible to capsize. The two cockpits make for fun double handed sailing. Once you're seated in WindRider's comfortable, enclosed cockpit, you'll find simple, intuitive controls: foot pedals for steering (tiller extensions are optional) and a mainsail sheet. You're free to sit back and watch the world rush by.

Nigel Iren's design SeaRail 19 

The SR-19 is an exciting, affordable, light-weight, trailerable trimaran built to cater to a wide rangel of sailors. Whether it's day saling with your family, resort sailing or camper sailing, the SeaRail 19 is an ideal leisure trimaran. However, If you have the need for speed try sailing this tri competitively with either friends or single handed. The SR-19 can be transferred from trailer to water or vice versa, ready to sail, in just 15 minutes!

RAPIDO Trimarans

Trimarans are fun because they enable you to discover places that others cannot venture to. The draft of the Rapido 60 is only 0.75m when the rudder blade and dagger board are retracted, which facilitates shallow water navigation and anchorage. Comfortable nets 6.3m x 3m on both sides of the Rapido 60 provide an enormous space to play or lounge when the fish are not biting
